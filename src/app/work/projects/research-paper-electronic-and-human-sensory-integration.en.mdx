## Overview

<div className="overview-text">

Regular quality control of Colombian cocoa liquor (CL) has historically been constrained by the limited availability of 
expert sensory panels and insufficient calibration between instrumental and sensory methodologies. This research developed 
a multimodal sensory dataset comprising 50 distinct CL samples by integrating human sensory evaluation (HSE) data from 187 
panelists with electronic sensory analysis (ESA) obtained using an electronic tongue (ET) and electronic nose (EN). To 
integrate these heterogeneous datasets, unprocessed instrumental signals were methodically transformed into comprehensible 
sensory descriptors. ET signals were converted into taste deviation scores utilizing the Approaching Target method in 
respect to a balanced CL reference, whilst EN time-series data underwent sensor reliability filtering, stationarity 
assessment, and cross-correlation with categorized scent standards. Both modalities were subsequently standardized to a 
common 29-descriptor sensory vocabulary and z-normalized across data, facilitating direct cross-modality and sample-level 
comparison. Unsupervised Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) clustering determined an ideal 12-component linked covariance structure
 according to the Bayesian Information Criterion. The alignment of ESA- and HSE-derived partitions across common descriptors 
 resulted in a Normalized Mutual Information (NMI) score of 0.554, signifying moderate concordance and illustrating that
  electronic sensing and human perception provide complementary, rather than redundant, representations of the cocoa 
  sensory space. While ESA conducted extensive chemical monitoring, encompassing sub-threshold volatile detection, 
  HSE focused on perceptually prominent flavor characteristics. The curated multimodal dataset provides a solid basis 
  for future machine learning applications, such as automatic flavor attribute classification and cocoa quality grade
   prediction. This hybrid approach facilitates scalable, objective, and market-relevant quality evaluation, 
providing practical value for data-driven quality control in Colombian cocoa production systems.

</div>

## Scope

- Comparative analysis between instrumental and human sensory signals.
- Modeling for alignment and interpretation of both sources.
